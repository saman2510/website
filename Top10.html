Top 10 Richest Countries In The World

  1. Singapore

    2. Singapore's economy is projected to grow between 1.0 to 3.0 percent in 2025, as per the Ministry of Trade and Industry (MTI). The nominal GDP in 2024 is estimated to be US$547.39 billion, according to Trading Economics. In the second quarter of 2025, Singapore's GDP grew by 4.3 percent year-on-year.

  2. Luxembourg

    3. Luxembourg's nominal GDP was $85.76 billion in 2023. This represents a 5.04% increase compared to the previous year, 2022. In 2023, Luxembourg's GDP (PPP) was estimated to be $89 billion.

  3. Ireland

    4. Ireland's GDP is estimated to be $696 billion in 2025 according to World Economics, in Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) terms. They also project an initial estimate of $745 billion for 2026. This figure is 11% higher than the official estimate from the World Bank.

  4. Qatar

    5. Qatar's current GDP is estimated at US$217.98 billion for 2024. The GDP per capita is around US$80,365, according to World Economics. Real GDP (2010 US$) is estimated at $176 billion with a per capita of $65,249.

  5. Norway

    6. Norway's nominal GDP is estimated to be around $506.470 billion (USD) in 2025, while its GDP in Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) is estimated at $597.420 billion. The country's economy is heavily reliant on natural resources, particularly oil and gas, and boasts one of the highest GDP per capita figures globally.

  6. Switzerland

    7. The economy of Switzerland is one of the world's most advanced and a highly-developed free market economy. The economy of Switzerland has ranked first in the world since 2015 on the Global Innovation Index[23][24] and third in the 2020 Global Competitiveness Report

  7. Brunei Darussalam

    8. Brunei Darussalam's nominal GDP for 2023 is estimated to be around $15.13 billion. This represents a slight increase of 1.41% compared to the previous year, 2022. However, when compared to 2022's GDP of $16.68 billion, there was a decline of 9.31%. Brunei's GDP is relatively small, accounting for only 0.4% of the Southeast Asian regional GDP.

  8. Guyana

    9. Guyana's GDP is experiencing rapid growth, primarily driven by its burgeoning oil and gas sector. Real GDP growth was estimated at 43.8% in 2024. This surge has transformed Guyana from one of the poorest countries in South America to one of the fastest-growing economies globally.

  9. United States

    10. The U.S. nominal GDP is currently at $29.962 trillion. In the first quarter of 2025, real GDP decreased at an annualized rate of 0.5 percent.

  10. Denmark

    11. Denmark's nominal GDP was approximately $429.46 billion USD in December 2024. In 2023, the nominal GDP was $407.09 billion USD. For 2024, the projected real GDP growth is 2.9%, according to the International Monetary Fund.
